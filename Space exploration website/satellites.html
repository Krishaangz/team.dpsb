Satellites

Satellites are human-made or natural objects that orbit planets, moons, or stars. They serve various purposes including communication, observation, navigation, and scientific research.

Categories of Satellites

Communication Satellites

These satellites relay signals for television, radio, internet, and phone services. Positioned in geostationary orbits, they ensure global coverage, facilitating long-distance communication across continents and oceans. They play a crucial role in modern telecommunications, broadcasting, and global connectivity

Weather Satellites

Weather satellites monitor atmospheric conditions, providing real-time data for weather forecasting. Equipped with imaging sensors, they track cloud cover, temperature, humidity, and storm systems. These satellites help predict weather patterns, track hurricanes, and monitor environmental changes, aiding disaster management and climate studies.

Navigation Satellites

Navigation satellites form the Global Positioning System (GPS), enabling precise location tracking worldwide. They orbit Earth, transmitting signals that allow GPS receivers to calculate positions. These satellites are essential for navigation in aviation, maritime, land transport, and military applications, enhancing accuracy and safety.

Earth Observation Satellites

Earth observation satellites monitor and capture images of the planet's surface. They track environmental changes, including deforestation, urbanization, agriculture, and natural disasters. These satellites provide critical data for climate research, land management, and disaster response, supporting scientific studies and resource management.

Scientific Satellites

Scientific satellites are designed to conduct experiments and gather data in space. These satellites study phenomena like cosmic radiation, solar activity, and dark matter. They help advance knowledge of space, astrophysics, and planetary science, contributing to a better understanding of the universe and Earth's place within it.

Military Satellites

Military satellites serve strategic defense and intelligence purposes. They provide secure communication, reconnaissance, and surveillance capabilities. These satellites support navigation, missile guidance, and national security operations, playing a key role in modern warfare by monitoring enemy activity and ensuring real-time communication for military force
