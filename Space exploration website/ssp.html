Planets of Our Solar System

Mercury

As the closest planet to the Sun, Mercury has extremely high temperatures and a thin atmosphere. It's known for its rapid orbit and small size.

Venus

With a thick, toxic atmosphere, Venus experiences a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet. It's known as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size.

Earth

Our home planet, Earth, is the only known planet with liquid water on its surface and the conditions to support life as we know it.

Mars

Often called the "Red Planet," Mars has a thin atmosphere and surface features reminiscent of both Earth and the Moon, with the possibility of ancient life.

Jupiter

The largest planet in our solar system, Jupiter is a gas giant with a massive storm system known as the Great Red Spot, persisting for centuries.

Saturn

Famous for its prominent ring system, Saturn is another gas giant with a fascinating array of moons and complex atmospheric phenomena.

Uranus

An ice giant with a striking blue-green color due to methane, Uranus orbits the Sun on its side, giving it a unique rotational axis.

Neptune

Known for its intense blue color and fierce storms, Neptune is the outermost planet and an ice giant with a dynamic atmosphere.

Pluto

Originally considered the ninth planet, Pluto was reclassified as a "dwarf planet" in 2006 due to its size and irregular orbit. The demotion sparked a global movement with the rallying cry: #JusticeForPluto.
